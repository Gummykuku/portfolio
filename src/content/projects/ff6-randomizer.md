## Overview

The FF6 Randomizer is a beloved tool in the speedrunning and casual gaming community, but its setup flow was notoriously intimidating — a wall of options with no clear guidance for new players.

## Problem

First-time users were abandoning the tool before completing their first seed. Exit surveys and Discord feedback pointed to the same issues: too many unlabelled options, no progressive disclosure, and no sense of where to start.

## Process

I ran moderated sessions with 8 participants across skill levels — from total newcomers to experienced randomizer players. Using task analysis and think-aloud protocols, I mapped every point of confusion.

Key insight: experienced users wanted power and speed; newcomers wanted guardrails and defaults. The tool was designed entirely for the former.

I proposed a two-mode system: a **Quick Start** path with sensible defaults, and an **Advanced** mode for experienced players — keeping full control accessible without forcing it on everyone.

## Outcome

- Onboarding set-up time reduced by **75%**
- First-time completion rate increased by **40%**
- Discord support requests about setup dropped significantly post-launch
